## Technological Details of the Bioprinting Processes

#### Jetting-Based Printing
Jetting-based printing is a high-resolution technique that is particularly suitable for creating detailed structures. This method involves the precise deposition of bioinks in a layer-by-layer process, allowing for the creation of intricate and complex tissues [15]. Jetting-based printing is often used for applications requiring high precision, such as skin grafts and blood vessels.

#### Extrusion-Based Printing
Extrusion-based printing is a versatile technique that can accommodate a wide range of bioinks, including those with high cell density and complex viscosities. This method is ideal for creating larger, more complex structures and is commonly used for bone and cartilage bioprinting [16]. The ability to print with a variety of bioinks makes extrusion-based printing a valuable tool in the development of 3D bioprinted tissues and organs.
